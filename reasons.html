Why do people migrate?

The reasons for migrating can be broadly categorized into push and pull factors. Pull factors are the positive attributes that attract people to a specific location, such as better job opportunities, higher living standards, and a safer environment. These factors encourage individuals to relocate in search of improved quality of life. On the other hand, push factors consist of negative elements that compel individuals to leave their current place of residence. These can include economic hardship, political instability, natural disasters, or lack of social services. Together, these push and pull factors play a crucial role in influencing migration patterns globally.
